  • Project 2

    Project Title: Seasonal Trends and Time Series Analysis of Monthly Airline Passenger Traffic (1949-1960)-RStudio

    Overview

    This report analyzes the Air Passengers dataset using various statistical tests and visualizations in RStudio. The dataset provides monthly totals of international airline passengers from 1949 to 1960. The analysis includes descriptive statistics, normality tests, hypothesis testing, regression analysis, and time series analysis, with detailed plots.

    Objectives

    To analyze monthly airline passenger data from 1949 to 1960, focusing on identifying trends and seasonal patterns, examining differences between periods, and evaluating overall changes in passenger traffic over time.

    Literature Review

    Time series analysis is essential for studying seasonal patterns in passenger traffic. Techniques like decomposition and regression help identify trends and seasonality. Hypothesis testing methods, such as t-tests and ANOVA, compare different periods or groups, while correlation tests assess variable relationships. Non-parametric tests address data not meeting parametric assumptions. This analysis utilizes these methods to explore the Air Passengers dataset, revealing trends, seasonality, and data relationships.

    Hypothesis

    1. The data exhibits significant seasonal variation.
    2. There is a significant difference in passenger numbers between the periods 1949-1954 and 1955-1960.
    3. There is a correlation between time and passenger numbers.
    4. The regression model accurately captures trends.

    Gaps

    The dataset lacks additional variables such as economic indicators or flight capacities, which could provide more insights into passenger trends. The reliability analysis, which is not suitable here, was omitted. Including more variables could enhance the depth of the analysis.

    Methodology

    Descriptive statistics summarize the dataset. Normality and hypothesis tests evaluate distributions and differences. Regression analysis assesses time trends. Time series decomposition identifies trend, seasonal, and random components. Plots include histograms, boxplots, scatter plots, and time series plots with enhanced details, including lines of best fit.

    Findings
    1. Descriptive Statistics

    The analysis showed that passenger counts range from 112 to 622, with a median of 265.5 and a mean of 280.3.

    2. Normality Test

    The Shapiro-Wilk test revealed that the data is not normally distributed, with a p-value less than 0.05.

    3. Independent T-Test

    A t-test indicated a significant difference in passenger numbers between the periods 1949-1954 and 1955-1960, with a p-value less than 0.05.

    4. ANOVA

    ANOVA results showed significant variation in passenger numbers across different years, with a p-value less than 0.05.

    5. Correlation Test

    Pearson correlation analysis found a significant positive correlation between time and passenger numbers (r = 0.92, p < 0.05).

    6. Regression Analysis

    The linear regression model confirmed a significant trend in passenger numbers over time, with a p-value less than 0.05 (p < 0.05).

    7. Non-Parametric Test

    The Wilcoxon rank-sum test confirmed significant differences between the periods, suitable for non-normal data.

    8. Time Series Analysis

    Decomposition identified clear trend and seasonal components in the time series data.

    1. Time Series Plot Plot

    The time series plot visualizes the monthly passenger counts over time, with overlaid trend and seasonal components from the time series decomposition to highlight underlying patterns and fluctuations.

    2. ARIMA Model Forecast Plot

    This plot shows the forecasted number of airline passengers for the next 12 months based on the ARIMA model fitted to historical data. The plot includes the observed monthly passenger counts (historical data) and the forecasted values, along with shaded confidence intervals that represent the uncertainty of the forecasts. The x-axis represents time, while the y-axis shows the number of passengers. The forecast helps visualize the predicted trends and potential future fluctuations in passenger traffic.

    3. Histogram

    The histogram illustrates the distribution of monthly passenger numbers, showing frequency of passenger counts within specified bins and a density line to highlight overall distribution trends.

    4. Density Plot

    The density plot, superimposed on the histogram, provides a smoothed estimate of the passenger count distribution, aiding in visualizing the data's overall distribution and density trends.

    5. Boxplot

    The boxplot displays the range, median, and interquartile range of passenger numbers across different years, highlighting any outliers and showing variations within and between years.

    6. Scatterplot

    The scatter plot depicts the relationship between time and passenger numbers, including a regression line to visualize the trend and direction of the relationship.

    7. Regression Line Plot

    The regression plot shows a scatter of monthly passenger numbers over time with a linear regression line, highlighting the overall trend and relationship between time and passenger counts.

    Hypothesis Testing

    1. Seasonal Variation: Accepted. The time series decomposition shows clear seasonal patterns with significant variation.
    2. Period Differences: Accepted. The t-test reveals a significant difference in passenger numbers between the periods 1949-1954 and 1955-1960.
    3. Correlation with Time: Accepted. The Pearson correlation test indicates a strong positive correlation between time and passenger numbers (r = 0.82, p < 0.05).
    4. Regression Model: Accepted. The regression model significantly captures the trend in passenger numbers over time (p < 0.05).

    Discussion

    The analysis confirms significant seasonal patterns and trends in passenger traffic. The Shapiro-Wilk test suggests the data is non-normally distributed, indicating that non-parametric methods might be necessary. The t-test and ANOVA highlight significant differences in passenger numbers across periods and years. The correlation and regression analyses reveal a strong relationship between time and passenger numbers. Time series decomposition further validates these findings by identifying trends and seasonal effects. Enhanced plots provide additional insights, showing data distribution and relationships clearly. The results align with established patterns in passenger traffic, although incorporating more variables could provide a deeper understanding.

    Hypothesis Testing

    2. Period Differences: Accepted. The t-test reveals a significant difference in passenger numbers between the periods 1949-1954 and 1955-1960.
    3. Correlation with Time: Accepted. The Pearson correlation test indicates a strong positive correlation between time and passenger numbers (r = 0.82, p < 0.05).

    Include additional variables like economic indicators or flight capacities for a more comprehensive analysis. Further research should focus on understanding seasonal effects and their drivers, which could offer a more nuanced view of passenger traffic trends.

    Conclusion

The regression model accurately captures these trends. While the analysis is insightful, adding more data variables could enhance the depth of understanding.
